Part 1: Data

Our data contains information about 651 titles of movies released from 1970 to 2014. We have some aspect of theses movies like the genre, the date of release, scores they receive from different sources, how well they were in the box office and other relevant information. Our main goal here is to test what features make a movie popular. This data gives us some relevant information to test our required question.

Part 2: Data manipulation

feature_film=NULL;
Drama=NULL;
mpaa_rating_R=NULL;
oscar_season=NULL;
summer_season=NULL;
for(i in 1:651){
       if(movies$title_type[i]=="Feature Film"){
         feature_film[i]="yes"
           }else{
          feature_film[i]="no"
          }
       if(movies$genre[i]=="Drama"){
         Drama[i]="yes"
           }else{
          Drama[i]="no"
          }
        if(movies$mpaa_rating[i]=="R"){
          mpaa_rating_R[i]="yes"
          }else{
          mpaa_rating_R[i]="no"
                }
        if(is.element(movies$thtr_rel_month[i],c(10,11,12))){
            oscar_season[i]="yes"
        }else{
           oscar_season[i]="no"
        }
        if(is.element(movies$thtr_rel_month[i],c(5,6,7,8))){
            summer_season[i]="yes"
        }else{
           summer_season[i]="no"
        }
           
} 
movies=movies %>% as.data.frame() %>%
mutate(Drama=Drama,mpaa_rating_R=mpaa_rating_R,oscar_season=oscar_season,summer_season=summer_season,feature_film=feature_film)

Part 6: Conclusion

After finding the model I have found out that the most effective variables for audience score are critics score and IMDB rating and after that runtime. We also could see that for an arbitrary movie, LaLa Land, our model could predict the real value of audience score. Therefore, we can say the attributes that make a movie popular are mostly critics score and IMDB rating.
